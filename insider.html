Full Experience
📅 If You Have 3 Days
The right rhythm — each day builds on the last.
1
Ancient Town + Food Tour
Orient yourself, eat everything

Start at 6:30am — the Ancient Town is almost empty. Walk the Japanese Bridge, photograph the yellow walls, find the morning market. By 9am you'll feel the city before the crowds arrive.

Afternoon: follow the food trail through Old Town. Bánh mì at Phượng, Cao Lầu at Thanh, finish with black sesame chè at Xi Ma Phụ.

Evening: lantern boat on the river, then a slow walk as the streets fill with lantern light.

🏮 Japanese Bridge 6:30am 🍜 Cao Lầu Thanh 🥖 Bánh Mì Phượng 🚣 Lantern Boat
2
Beach + Coconut Forest + Cooking Class
The best of countryside Hội An

Morning cooking class at Cocolocal — market visit, farm pick, hands-on cooking. Done by noon with a full meal and new skills.

Afternoon: coconut boat ride through the nipa palm waterways, then straight to An Bàng for the beach. Stay for sunset cocktails at Seascape.

🍳 Cocolocal Cooking Class 🥥 Coconut Boat 🏖 An Bàng Beach 🌅 Sunset Cocktails
3
My Sơn Sanctuary or Cham Island
Pick one — both are full days

My Sơn: Ancient Hindu temples of the Cham Kingdom, hidden in jungle. About 1 hour from Hội An. Go early, stay for the sunrise light through the ruins.

Cham Island: Crystal water, coral reefs, snorkeling, fresh seafood lunch on the island. Full-day boat trip — best on a sunny, calm-weather day.

🏛 My Sơn — jungle temples 🏝 Cham Island — coral reefs
Eat Like a Local
🍜 Top 5 Foods You Must Try
Many visitors leave Hội An without trying its most iconic dishes. Don't be one of them.
1
Cao Lầu
Thick chewy noodles, char siu pork, crispy cracklings, and fresh herbs. Made with water from a specific local well — the recipe doesn't travel.
📍 Cao Lầu Thanh Only in Hội An
2
White Rose Dumplings
Delicate steamed rice paper dumplings shaped like white roses, filled with shrimp. Soft, subtle, beautiful.
Hội An specialty
3
Bánh Mì
Hội An's version is different — crispy baguette, generous fillings, the unmistakable house sauce. Anthony Bourdain called it the best in the world.
📍 Bánh Mì Phượng 📍 Madam Khánh
4
Black Sesame Sweet Soup
Rich, smooth, slightly herbal — a traditional Hội An dessert most tourists walk right past without knowing it exists.
📍 Xí Mà Phụ
5
Cơm Gà — Chicken Rice
A local classic constantly overlooked by tourists. Yellow turmeric rice, shredded chicken, fresh herbs and dipping sauce. Simple, perfect.

🤫 Local Secrets
The things we tell guests when they arrive — not published anywhere.
Ancient Town
Best time to walk Old Town
⏰ 6:00 – 8:00 AM
Almost empty streets. Cool weather. The best photos. By 9am the tour groups arrive and the magic fades. Set your alarm.
An Bàng Beach
Best beach time
🌅 4:00 – 6:00 PM
Avoid midday heat entirely. The 4pm light is perfect for photos, the breeze picks up, and the sunset cocktail window opens at 5pm sharp.
Shopping
Tailor tip
Visit your tailor on Day 1, not your last day. You'll need 48–72 hours for fittings before departure. Bring reference photos. Ask us for a coupon.
Coffee
Egg coffee is a must-try
Vietnamese egg coffee — rich egg yolk custard over strong coffee. Strange name, unforgettable taste. Our favorite spot: 📍 Rao Café.
